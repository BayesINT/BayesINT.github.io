Goals of the Program

 
This program will bring statisticians and nuclear practitioners together to explore how Bayesian inference can enable progress on the frontiers of nuclear physics and open up new directions for the field.

 
Goals:

  • facilitate cross communication, fertilization, and collaboration on Bayesian applications among the nuclear sub-fields;
  • provide the opportunity for nuclear physicists who are unfamiliar with Bayesian methods to start applying them to new problems;
  • learn from the experts about innovative and advanced uses of Bayesian statistics, and best practices in applying them;
  • learn about advanced computational tools and methods;
  • critically examine the application of Bayesian methods to particular physics problems in the various subfields.
We're aiming to build a knowledge base, so we are working to enhance continuity across the program. Your suggestions are very much welcomed.
 

Speaker Instructions

For physicists:
  • Provide a high-level (=pedagogical) introduction to both the basic science questions you are trying to answer and an introduction to the statistical methods you are using (Bayesian or not). Please avoid jargon and remember that *most* attendees will not be experts in your field!
  • To help get your Bayesian statistical colleagues better engaged, be sure to describe features of the data, models, and possibly other information that is being used. Also explain the goals of your investigation: Are you focused on estimating key parameters? Making predictions? Choosing between competing models? Seeking understanding? Also, special features of your investigation will be important to present (e.g. computationally demanding models, data quality).

Speaker Instructions

For statisticians:
  • At the beginning of each week, a statistician will give an overview talk of Bayesian methods, with the aim of introducing common language and highlighting current research trends. The speakers should avoid unnecessary jargon and remember that most attendees may not be experts in applied mathematics or statistics. For the rest of the week, there will be a couple of talks each day on physics applications that could potentially make use of Bayesian statistics. The attending statisticians will be expected to engage in these talks, participate in discussions with physicist at the workshop, working out potential paths forward for some of these applications, and possibly developing future collaborations. There may be utility in informally presenting some of your statistical work during afternoon discussions.

Other Items

  • Seminar etiquette: interrupt freely! May need to defer some questions / discussion to afternoon to allow speaker to finish
  • If notation or terminology is unfamiliar, please ask!
  • Lunches and dinners are self-organized
